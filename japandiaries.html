dear japan...

I have been studying the Japanese language since 9th grade. It is actually my 3rd language after Korean, which I studied on my own (and can now barely speak). Originally, I always wanted to study Korean in a proper educational setting, but upon entering university, I found myself already too deep into Japanese.

In 2017, I travelled to Japan for the first time with my high school Japanese language teacher and a small group of classmates. That trip changed my life; I fell in love with the beautiful country and have become interested in working there professionally in the future. Since then, I have been keen on returning to Japan, and I did two years later.

But another two weeks wasn't enough. I dream of one day visiting all 47 prefectures (similar to states or provinces) of Japan, but if two weeks could only cover a small part of Tokyo, how long would such a journey last?

Follow my journey through this online documentation of my travels to Japan!

The second trip to Japan: Tokyo June 2019

This trip was completely funded by and planned by me and my boyfriend. I felt so proud of ourselves for succeeding in returning to Japan (he was on the previous trip as well). We spent two weeks in Tokyo, and, afraid we would run out of activities, kept some day trip ideas in mind. However, we never ended up having time for excursions outside of Tokyo, a metropolis too large and interesting to explore within two weeks - even months maybe! I kept a journal during this trip, which will guide future in-depth posts about this trip! I also went on this trip with the intentions of creating a video montage for it, whereas I had the idea of creating a video for the 2017 trip after having already come back to Seattle.

The first trip to Japan: Kyoto, Shizuoka, & Tokyo Summer 2017

This trip was planned by my teacher and a travel agency. Upon arriving at Narita Airport in Tokyo, we took a connecting flight to Itami Airport in Osaka, where we stayed for one night before moving to Kyoto the next day. We stayed in Kyoto for two days before transferring to Shizuoka by bullet train, where we experienced attending a Japanese high school and stayed with local families. Our last stop of the trip was four days in Tokyo.
